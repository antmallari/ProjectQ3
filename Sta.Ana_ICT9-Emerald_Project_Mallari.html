How to Prevent Fire
Accessible Equipment Ensure that all of your fire safety supplies, such as control panels and extinguishers, are easily accessible. Additionally, avoid covering anything, such as dust, debris, or paint, with the fire sprinklers or fire alarms. You don't want to make your equipment less functional.
Proper Disposal Any hazardous waste should be disposed of in a metal container with a lid. Anything from oils to chemicals can be considered hazardous waste. To avoid fire hazards, all materials, including those that are flammable and combustible, should be disposed of properly.
Regular Maintenance To ensure that all of your fire protection equipment is up to code, schedule routine maintenance services. Additionally, it's a good idea to keep all of the equipment in your building well-maintained to avoid overheating or friction-related sparks.
Safe Storage Make sure they are kept in a secure location if your building contains chemicals, flammable materials, or other hazardous materials. Ensure they are in a dry, safe space, such as a closet or room, with enough ventilation. Just in case, it's a good idea to keep flammable substance fire suppression gear close by the storage area.
Clean Environment You should maintain a clean and organized building for a variety of reasons. One of the main causes is that a fire can spread more quickly when there is a lot of clutter, particularly flammable materials like paper, boxes, and other items. In the event of a fire, the clutter may also block exits and make it more difficult to escape. Therefore, be sure to maintain a tidy and clutter-free interior.
